The Enochian Calendar

Revealed to Enoch by the angel Uriel (1 Enoch 72–82). A 364-day solar year divided into four seasons of exactly 13 weeks each — perfectly divisible by 7, so every Sabbath falls on the same weekday forever.

Month Structure
Each season has 3 months:
Month 1 — 30 days
Month 2 — 30 days
Month 3 — 30 days + 1 intercalary ★

The intercalary day is outside the month count — it is a "day of the Lord" marking the season boundary. Total: 91 days per season × 4 = 364.
vs. Lunar Calendar
The lunar year has 354 days — 10 days short of the Enochian year. Jubilees 6:36 warns: "They will go astray as to new moons, seasons, sabbaths and festivals." The Qumran community broke from the Jerusalem Temple partly over this calendar dispute.

The Luminaries — 1 Enoch 72–82

The Astronomical Book (also called the Book of the Luminaries) is possibly the oldest section of 1 Enoch, dating to the 3rd century BCE. The angel Uriel reveals to Enoch the precise laws governing the sun, moon, and stars — the divine machinery behind the 364-day sacred calendar.
